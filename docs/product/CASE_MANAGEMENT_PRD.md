# Case Management Product Requirements Document (PRD)

**Phase:** 7
**Project:** ASTRA (Adaptive Security Threat Response & Automation Platform)
**Status:** Approved

## 1. Purpose of Cases
The ASTRA Case Management module bridges the gap between automated detection (Correlation & Observation Engines) and human-led investigation and response. A "Case" in ASTRA is a structured, immutable workspace that aggregates related Observations, Evidence, and timeline events to provide Security Analysts with a centralized view of a specific threat or incident. Its primary purpose is to facilitate deterministic resolution tracking, manual mitigation, and post-incident reporting.

## 2. Target Users
- **Security Analyst (Tier 1/2):** Primary users who triage cases, review evidence, execute manual mitigation steps, and update case status.
- **Security Responder (Tier 3):** Escalation point for complex cases; utilizes deeper evidence links and manual automation overrides.
- **SOC Manager:** Monitors case queues, reviews analyst performance (SLA tracking), and manages workload distribution.
- **Compliance/Audit Officer:** Reviews case resolution details, chain-of-custody for evidence, and historical timelines for audits.

## 3. Primary Use Cases
- **Automated Case Generation:** A high-severity Observation generated by the Policy Engine automatically spawns a Case.
- **Manual Case Escalation:** An Analyst manually upgrades an anomaly or low-level Observation into a formal Case.
- **Evidence Aggregation:** The system links related Evidence and Audit Events to the Case automatically to preserve chain of custody.
- **Collaborative Triage:** Multiple users comment, tag, and update the Case Timeline simultaneously.
- **Response Orchestration:** Security Responders trigger Automation actions directly from the Case context.

## 4. Case Lifecycle Goals
- **Determinism:** State transitions must be strictly enforced (e.g., a case cannot move from "Open" to "Closed" without passing through "Resolved").
- **Immutability:** The Case Timeline must serve as a tamper-proof record of every human and system action.
- **Efficiency:** Reduce Mean Time to Acknowledge (MTTA) and Mean Time to Respond (MTTR) by clearly presenting actionable data over raw noise.
- **Accountability:** Every Case must have a defined owner before mitigation begins.

## 5. Success Metrics
- **MTTA Reduction:** Decrease the time from Case creation to Assignment.
- **MTTR Reduction:** Decrease the time from Investigating to Resolved.
- **Escalation Accuracy:** 100% of high-severity Observations correctly spawn Cases without human intervention.
- **Audit Compliance:** 100% of Case state changes have a corresponding immutable Audit Event.

## 6. Out-of-Scope Items
- **Full SOAR Playbook Editor:** The UI for building complex Playbooks remains out of scope for Phase 7.
- **Multi-Tenant External Portals:** Exposing Cases directly to external customers or third parties.
- **Custom Case Fields Builder:** Allowing users to dynamically create new database schemas for Cases.
- **Standalone Ticketing System:** ASTRA Case Management is deeply integrated into the ASTRA ecosystem; it is not a general-purpose IT service desk replacement (e.g., Jira, ServiceNow).

## 7. Future Expansion Areas (Future AI Readiness)
- **AI-Assisted Triage:** Using the AI Reasoner to recommend priority and summarize the Case timeline.
- **Automated Assignee Recommendation:** AI recommending the best Analyst based on historical workload and expertise.
- **Drafting Executive Summaries:** Utilizing Gemini to draft the final post-incident report before Case closure.
- **Note on AI Limitations:** AI will never automatically close cases, override RBAC policies, or mutate evidence. All AI actions must be strictly bounded and require human approval.
